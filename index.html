About

Scribe is a Chrome extension that replaces raw URLS in Gmail compose and reply windows to human-friendly hyperlinks and images automatically. Links become the title text of the page they point to; image URLs become the image they represent. These features were inspired by Quora's text editor.

Just type in the raw URL, click outside the document, press F2, and wait.

Version

Scribe is at version 1.0.

Things You Should Know

  • Scribe is safe. Images and links are embedded using a secure HTTPS middleman.
  • Scribe is sensible. It preserves formatting, works across all open reply and compose windows, and will turn a URL into a hyperlink if it can't find the image or page it can't find.
  • Scribe is useful. Why use Google Chrome's inbuilt editor, when you can do it at the press of a button?

Scribe's speed depends on your network connection.

Things We Want to Fix

  • Right now, Scribe makes synchronous requests. That means you can't do anything else in Gmail until it finishes loading your images and hyperlinks. We don't like that behaviour any more than you do.

  • There's no obvious notification system / animation right now to let you know when we're turning your URLs into something pretty. We're working on this right now.
